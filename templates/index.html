Phi Sigma Pi

is a national, coeducational honor fraternity that blends the best features of academic honor societies, service organizations, and social fraternities into one dynamic student organization. Founded in 1916, Phi Sigma Pi has grown to 103 collegiate chapters with approximately 5,000 undergraduate members and over 25,710 alumni members. Phi Sigma Pi is an active organization. We host educational programs, service projects, and social events. On the national level, we have conventions, banquets, and grand chapter meetings to unite all brothers in Phi Sigma Pi.

"Delta Xi became a Chapter of Phi Sigma Pi National Honor Fraternity on December 3rd, 2000. That Fall, University of Missouri Director of Expansion, Hoyt Craver, sent letters out to all MU students with at least a 3.0 GPA inviting them to attend a "Meet Phi Sigma Pi Night." The next night, all interested students attended the very first chapter meeting; thus, establishing and becoming the Founding Fathers of Delta Xi. Since then, Delta Xi has continued to grow and was recognized at the 2010 National Convention as one of the largest Chapters in the country. Since our recognition as a Chapter of Phi Sigma Pi National Honor Fraternity, Delta Xi has strived to uphold our tripod of ideals of Scholarship, Leadership, and Fellowship. We have actively volunteered in our community with organizations such as Cedar Creek Therapeutic Riding Center, Central Missouri Food Bank, the Humane Society, Clean-up Columbia, and many others. Additionally, we hold educational and social events such as meeting up as a group for dinner, a scavenger hunt similar to the "Amazing Race", trivia nights, trips to Venture Out, and much more." - Jerome Link, 2011-2012 Delta Xi Chapter President
